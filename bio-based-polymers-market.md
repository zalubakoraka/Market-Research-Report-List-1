<p><p>The bio-based polymers market is segmented into two main types: biodegradable and non-degradable. Biodegradable polymers are designed to break down naturally through microbial action, making them suitable for applications like packaging and agricultural films. They offer environmental benefits by reducing plastic waste. In contrast, non-degradable bio-based polymers, while derived from renewable sources, do not degrade easily and are used in more durable applications like automotive and construction materials, where longevity and strength are prioritized.</p></p>
