<p><p>The hand-held plenoptic camera market features two primary types: standard plenoptic cameras and focused plenoptic cameras. Standard plenoptic cameras capture light fields, allowing users to refocus images post-capture, making them ideal for general photography and creative applications. Focused plenoptic cameras, on the other hand, emphasize depth and detail, offering higher resolution and improved image quality for specific tasks, such as scientific imaging or industrial inspection. Both types provide distinct advantages, catering to diverse consumer needs and preferences in photography.</p></p>
