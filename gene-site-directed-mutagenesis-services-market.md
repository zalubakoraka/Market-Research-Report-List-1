<p><p>The Gene Site-Directed Mutagenesis Services market encompasses several types of mutations: synonymous mutations, missense mutations, and nonsense mutations. Synonymous mutations alter nucleotide sequences without changing the protein product, allowing researchers to study gene function without affecting protein function. Missense mutations, which change one amino acid in the protein sequence, can affect protein structure and activity, making them useful for functional studies. Nonsense mutations introduce premature stop codons, leading to truncated proteins, and are vital for investigating loss-of-function scenarios in genetic research.</p></p>
