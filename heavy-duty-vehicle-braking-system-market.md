<p><p>The heavy-duty vehicle braking system market primarily includes two types: drum brakes and disc brakes. Drum brakes consist of a hollow drum that rotates with the wheel, where brake shoes press against the inner surface to create friction. They are known for their durability and cost-effectiveness. Disc brakes, on the other hand, utilize rotors and calipers, providing superior heat dissipation and quicker response times. Both systems are essential for ensuring safety and performance in heavy-duty vehicles, each catering to specific operational needs.</p></p>
